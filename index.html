★ exereunesis · add-on teardown · 1994 ★

A mushroom-shaped cap that plugged into the Genesis cartridge slot, the 32X strapped two Hitachi SH-2 processors onto the 16-bit machine as a stopgap before the Saturn. It split Sega's audience, confused buyers, and was abandoned within months — a cautionary tale of bridging hardware.

The Idea

the three-beat story

Two SH-2s on a Cap
the architecture

The 32X adds two Hitachi SH-2 CPUs at 23 MHz and its own video processor, plugged into the Genesis cartridge slot and overlaying graphics on the host's output. Real extra power, awkwardly bolted on.

A Bridge Too Far
the strategy

Sega meant it as a cheap step toward 32-bit gaming before the Saturn arrived — but launching a stopgap months before a flagship split the market and the message, and developers had little reason to support it.

A Tower of Sega
the mess

Stack a 32X on a Genesis with a Sega CD beneath and you had three boxes, three power supplies, and a tangle of cables — the emblem of Sega's fragmented early-90s hardware strategy.
